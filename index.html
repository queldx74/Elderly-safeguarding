What is Safeguarding?

“Safeguarding means protecting an adult’s right to live in safety, free from abuse and neglect.”

It is the responsibility of all adults — family, friends, neighbours, professionals — to act when they believe an older person is at risk of harm, has already been harmed, or is being abused or neglected.

Safeguarding applies to any adult (18 or over) who has care and support needs, and who is experiencing, or is at risk of, abuse or neglect.

Legal Basis

Care Act 2014 — Statutory guidance

Who It Covers

Adults with care & support needs

Core Principle

Making safeguarding personal

Types of Abuse

Physical

Hitting, slapping, misuse of medication

Psychological

Threats, humiliation, emotional coercion

Financial

Theft, fraud, misuse of property

Neglect

Ignoring medical needs, withholding care

Online

Scams, phishing, identity exploitation

Sexual

Any activity without consent

Discriminatory

Abuse due to race, gender, disability

Institutional

Poor care practices in settings

Warning Signs to Watch For

Sign 01 of 07

Unexplained Injuries or Bruising

Bruises, burns, or cuts that cannot be adequately explained, especially in unusual locations.
